I studied biology at the Ruhr-University Bochum and the Eberhard-Karls University of Tuebingen. For my PhD I joined the Department of Neurophysiology at the Ruhr-University Bochum to study learning and memory in the olfactory system. During that time, I was associated to the International Graduate School of Neuroscience and part of the Research School of the Ruhr-University Bochum. I continued and extended my research interests during my current post-doc time. Besides my research work, I am involved in the teaching of physiology in the medical faculty.

My research aims to investigate the role of synaptic plasticity, as the basis for learning and memory, in regions of the olfactory system, associated brain regions and the hippocampus. The examination of how olfactory sensory information are encoded in the form of synaptic plasticity in the piriform cortex is one of my main research interests. Furthermore, I am interested in finding out how and in which regions olfactory information is processed and stored into long-term memories in other olfactory regions, associated brain regions and the hippocampus.
